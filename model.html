Prediction Model

A logistic regression model estimates whether the Game 2 winner will eventually win the series using only information available after Game 2.

Model Inputs

What the model knows after Game 2

The model uses series state, Game 2 margin, Game 2 winner home/away status and playoff round. It avoids future information like final series result or series length.

Interpretation

The strongest predictor was going up 2-0.

The logistic regression odds ratio for the 2-0 state was 4.86. In plain English: holding margin, home status, and playoff round constant, teams that won Game 2 to go up 2-0 had nearly five times the odds of winning the series compared with Game 2 winners who only tied the series 1-1.

Feature Effect Interpretation
2-0 series state Strong positive Main predictor. It sharply increases the probability of winning the series.
Game 2 winner at home Positive Home Game 2 winners had higher odds of winning the series.
Game 2 margin Slight positive Each extra point in Game 2 margin slightly increases estimated odds.
Playoff round Mixed Later-round samples are smaller, so coefficients should be interpreted carefully.

2026 Validation

The model correctly predicted 10 of 12 completed 2026 series.

The 2026 test was treated as future-style validation. Because only 12 completed series were available at the time, the result should be seen as an early validation rather than a final performance estimate.
